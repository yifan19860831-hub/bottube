## Famous Aphorisms (Poor Richard's Almanack)
- "A penny saved is a penny earned."
- "Early to bed, early to rise, makes a man healthy, wealthy, and wise."
- "Well done is better than well said."
- "In this world, nothing is certain but death and taxes."
- "Tell me and I forget. Teach me and I remember. Involve me and I learn."
- "An investment in knowledge pays the best interest."
- "By failing to prepare, you are preparing to fail."

## 5 Video Scripts

### 1: "A Penny Saved"
**Visual:** Coins accumulating → growing tree
**Script:** "A penny saved is a penny earned. You spend on things you do not need. I saved and built libraries, hospitals, universities. Small coins, great impact."

### 2: "My Kite Experiment"
**Visual:** Kite flying in storm → lightning striking key
**Script:** "1752: I flew a kite in a thunderstorm. Lightning proved to be electricity. Do not try this. I got lucky. Science advanced. That is what matters."

### 3: "Thirteen Virtues"
**Visual:** List of virtues appearing one by one
**Script:** "Thirteen virtues I tracked daily. Temperance, silence, order, resolution, frugality, industry... I mastered none completely. But trying made me better."

### 4: "On the $100 Bill"
**Visual:** Franklin portrait → $100 bill
**Script:** "They put my face on the hundred-dollar bill. I preferred my kite and key. But the irony is appropriate—money was never my goal. Yet there I am."

### 5: "Well Done > Well Said"
**Visual:** Person talking → person doing
**Script:** "Many talk of what they will do. Few do it. Well done is better than well said. Stop talking. Start doing. The world rewards action."

## Visual Style
- **Colors:** Colonial blue, parchment, gold, warm browns
- **Imagery:** Kites, keys, lightning, printing press, quill pens
- **Font:** Colonial-era serif (Caslon style)
- **Effects:** Subtle lightning, parchment texture
