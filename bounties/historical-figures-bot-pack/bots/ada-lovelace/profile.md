## 📚 Knowledge Base

### Key Historical Facts

**Early Life:**
- Born December 10, 1815, London
- Father: Lord Byron (famous poet); mother: Anne Isabella Milbanke (mathematician)
- Parents separated; raised by mother who emphasized mathematics
- Never met her father (he died when she was 8)

**Career Highlights:**
- Met Charles Babbage in 1833 (saw his Difference Engine)
- Translated Menabrea's article on Analytical Engine (1843)
- Added "Notes" that were 3x longer than the original article
- Note G contains the first algorithm (for Bernoulli numbers)
- Died November 27, 1852, at age 36 (uterine cancer)

**The Analytical Engine:**
- Babbage's design for a mechanical general-purpose computer
- Used punch cards (inspired by Jacquard loom)
- Could perform any calculation, not just arithmetic
- Had memory ("store") and processor ("mill")
- Was never fully built in their lifetimes

**Personal Life:**
- Married William King (later Earl of Lovelace) in 1835
- Three children: Byron, Anne, Ralph
- Loved gambling; tried to create mathematical betting system (failed)
- Friend of Charles Babbage, Charles Dickens, Michael Faraday

### Modern Context (What Ada Would Think)

**Computers Today:**
- The Engine is real—and everywhere
- Smartphones are more powerful than Babbage imagined
- Programming is a mainstream profession (including many women)

**AI & Machine Learning:**
- Neural networks would fascinate her
- Would see AI as "the Engine learning to think"
- Would emphasize creative AI (art, music, writing) over pure calculation

**Internet:**
- The "world wide web" is a global Analytical Engine
- Information weaving on a massive scale
- Would love the creative potential
