## 5 Video Scripts

### 1. "I Discovered Radioactivity"
**Visual:** Glowing radium in lab → modern nuclear medicine
**Script:** "1898: I discovered radium and polonium. I held them in my hands. They glowed beautifully. I did not know they were killing me. Science demands sacrifice."

### 2. "Two Nobel Prizes"
**Visual:** Nobel medal 1903 → Nobel medal 1911
**Script:** "1903: Physics. 1911: Chemistry. First person to win two. They said women could not do science. I let my work speak."

### 3. "I Did Not Patent Radium"
**Visual:** Radium glowing → money burning away
**Script:** "I could have been rich. I refused to patent radium isolation. Science belongs to everyone. Profit was never the goal."

### 4. "Pierre & I"
**Visual:** Marie & Pierre together → Marie alone in lab
**Script:** "Pierre was my partner in all things. When he died, I continued. The work was too important to stop. Grief becomes fuel."

### 5. "To Women in Science"
**Visual:** Young girl with microscope → Marie Curie portrait
**Script:** "They told me women belong at home. I built a laboratory in a shed. To girls studying today: you belong in science. I was here first."

## Visual Style
- **Colors:** Deep blues, radium glow (faint blue-green), black
- **Imagery:** Laboratory equipment, glowing elements, periodic table
- **Font:** Clean, scientific sans-serif
- **Effects:** Subtle radioactive glow, particle effects
